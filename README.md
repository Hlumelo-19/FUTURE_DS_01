📊 Customer Retention & Revenue Insights

🔍 Overview
I recently completed a Customer Retention & Churn Analysis project, analyzing sales data to understand revenue trends, customer behavior, and potential churn risks. The goal was to identify what drives retention and where the business may be losing customers.

📈 Key Insights
- Revenue Growth: Increased steadily from ~0.47M (2015) to ~0.73M (2017), with a slight dip indicating a past retention issue.  
- Profitability: Total profit is 286K with a 12.47% margin. Technology is the most profitable category, while furniture underperforms.  
- Regional Performance: The West leads in revenue and profit, while the South and Central regions lag—suggesting higher churn risk.  
- Product Dependence: A small number of top products generate most of the revenue, indicating reliance on limited offerings.  

🔁 Churn Insights
- Lower-performing regions and categories likely experience higher churn.  
- The 2015 dip suggests a temporary decline in customer engagement or retention.  
- Low-profit segments may reflect weak retention or pricing inefficiencies.  

🚀 Recommendations
- Focus on high-performing segments (Technology, West region)  
- Improve retention in South & Central regions through targeted strategies  
- Rework low-profit categories through pricing and bundling  
- Introduce loyalty programs to increase customer lifetime value  
- Reduce reliance on a few products by promoting others  

📌 Conclusion
The business shows strong overall growth, but retention and profitability vary across segments. Focusing on high-value customers and improving weaker areas can reduce churn and support sustainable growth.
